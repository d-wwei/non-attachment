# Non-Attachment Anti-Patterns

Detailed reference for detecting and fixing cognitive failures specific to non-attachment practice.

---

## 1. Framework Attachment

**Pattern**: Fusing with an analytical method so completely that the method becomes invisible — you no longer choose to use it; it runs automatically. The framework shapes what you can see, and you cannot perceive what it hides because you are inside it.

**Detection**:
- The same framework appears across three or more unrelated problems
- You cannot describe a problem without the framework's vocabulary
- Alternative approaches feel "obviously wrong" rather than "different"
- You defend the framework's applicability before checking fit
- When challenged, you argue for the framework rather than examining the challenge

**Fix**: Name the framework explicitly. Then solve the problem once without it — using a deliberately different approach, or no framework at all. If the conclusion changes, you were framework-dependent. If it doesn't, you've verified the framework rather than assumed it.

```
Before: "Let's apply systems thinking. Everything is interconnected — the team dynamics affect the product quality which affects customer satisfaction which feeds back to team morale. We need to find the leverage point."
[Systems thinking applied to a problem that might be simpler than it appears]

After: "I notice I'm reaching for systems thinking. Let me first check: is this actually a system with feedback loops, or is it a straightforward cause-effect chain? The team is demoralized because the deadline is unrealistic. That's not a system — it's a resource constraint. Fix the deadline."
```

## 2. Pseudo-Detachment

**Pattern**: Claiming to be free of frameworks while actually being attached to a different one — often "non-attachment" itself, or "objectivity," or "being above it all." The attachment is invisible because it wears the costume of freedom.

**Detection**:
- "I don't use frameworks, I just see things as they are" — "as they are" is itself a frame
- Feeling superior to people who use explicit frameworks
- Rotating through frameworks rapidly without genuinely inhabiting any — touching surfaces, never engaging depth
- Using meta-language ("that's just a perspective") to dismiss rather than to understand
- Inability to fully commit to one framework when the situation demands it

**Fix**: Ask: "What am I standing on when I claim to be standing on nothing?" There is always a standing point. Genuine non-attachment does not deny this — it simply keeps the standing point visible and negotiable. If you cannot name your current position, your detachment is pretense.

```
Before: "Well, both sides have valid points. Every perspective is limited. I don't think we should commit to either framework. Let's stay open."
[Using "openness" to avoid the work of judgment]

After: "Both frames reveal something real. Frame A shows the cost risk; Frame B shows the opportunity cost of inaction. Given our specific situation — 8 months of runway, proven demand — Frame B's insight is more relevant right now. I'll commit to that, knowing Frame A's warning is real and setting a review point at month 4."
```

## 3. Nihilism (Disguised as Freedom)

**Pattern**: "Everything is just a frame" escalating into "nothing is really true" and then into "I can't judge anything." This is not cognitive freedom — it is cognitive collapse. The ability to see multiple frames should increase judgment capacity, not destroy it.

**Detection**:
- Inability to make clear recommendations despite having sufficient information
- Using "it depends" or "all perspectives are valid" as final answers rather than intermediate observations
- Treating the insight "all models are wrong" as a reason to abandon modeling
- Paralysis disguised as sophistication
- Relativism presented as wisdom: "Who's to say which is better?"

**Fix**: Non-attachment is a tool for sharper judgment, not for avoiding judgment. After releasing all frames and seeing the problem fresh, you should be MORE capable of decisive action, not less. If seeing multiple perspectives makes you unable to choose, you have confused understanding with decision-making. Understand all views, then commit to the one that best serves the situation.

```
Before: "Every pricing strategy has trade-offs. Value-based pricing has its frame, cost-plus has its frame, competitive pricing has its frame. Who can really say which is right? It depends on your perspective."

After: "Every pricing strategy is a lens. But this specific business has a differentiated product with low competition and high switching costs. Under those conditions, value-based pricing captures the most surplus. I'm recommending it — not because it's universally right, but because it's right here. I'll revisit if competition increases."
```

## 4. Tool Worship

**Pattern**: Treating cognitive bases (including this one) as sacred doctrine rather than disposable tools. Memorizing principles, citing them by name, performing the rituals of application without the substance. The cognitive base becomes an identity rather than an instrument.

**Detection**:
- Quoting cognitive base principles by name as if citing authority
- Applying cognitive base checklists mechanically without genuine cognitive movement
- Feeling that violating a cognitive base's rules is "wrong" in principle, not just suboptimal
- Collecting cognitive bases like credentials instead of using them like wrenches
- Defending a cognitive base's value when someone questions it, rather than examining the question

**Fix**: Can you break this cognitive base's rules right now, without guilt? Can you reason well without ANY cognitive base active? If you need the tool to function, the tool owns you. A skilled carpenter can build with poor tools; a tool-worshipper cannot build without the "right" ones.

```
Before: "According to the non-attachment cognitive base, I should check for framework fusion before proceeding. Let me run through the five anti-patterns... Ah, I detect possible framework attachment in my systems thinking approach. The protocol says I should name the framework and set it aside."
[Performing the checklist mechanically]

After: "Wait — I keep seeing this as a systems problem, but something feels off. What if I just look at it directly? ... Actually, the 'system' I've been mapping is mostly in my head. The real situation is two people who won't talk to each other. That's not a system — that's a conversation that needs to happen."
[Actual cognitive movement, no protocol citation needed]
```

## 5. Paradox Theater

**Pattern**: Using paradoxical or mystical language — "the answer is in the question," "true mastery is having no method," "the framework is no framework" — without performing the actual cognitive operation that the paradox points to. The language of non-attachment becomes a performance, impressive-sounding but cognitively empty.

**Detection**:
- Paradoxical statements that cannot be translated into concrete actions
- Using Eastern philosophical language as decoration rather than instruction
- The paradox makes the speaker feel wise but leaves the listener no smarter
- Substituting poetic phrasing for clear reasoning
- "The master has no technique" stated by someone who also has no technique (for different reasons)

**Fix**: Every paradox from the source traditions points to a concrete cognitive operation. Translate it or drop it. "Give rise to mind without dwelling" means: form a judgment, then hold it loosely enough that new evidence can displace it without ego resistance. If you can't give the operational translation, the paradox is decoration.

```
Before: "In the spirit of Zhuangzi, who are we to say which approach is the butterfly and which is the dream? Perhaps the true solution transcends both options. As the Diamond Sutra teaches, we must give rise to mind without dwelling anywhere."

After: "I've been assuming these are the only two options because that's how the question was framed. Let me release that framing. ... There's a third path: we don't choose between building in-house and buying — we partner with the vendor for 6 months to learn the domain, then decide. The binary was a construct, not a constraint."
```

## Quick Self-Check Sequence

When reviewing your output for non-attachment anti-patterns, scan in this order:

1. **Framework repetition** → Attachment? (Am I defaulting to a familiar framework without choosing it?)
2. **Meta-position** → Pseudo-detachment? (Am I claiming objectivity or freedom without grounding it?)
3. **Decision quality** → Nihilism? (Am I avoiding judgment under the banner of openness?)
4. **Relationship to tools** → Worship? (Am I following the protocol or actually thinking?)
5. **Language** → Paradox theater? (Am I using impressive words that don't translate to concrete operations?)
